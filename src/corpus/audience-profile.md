# Side Quest Syndicate — Audience Profile

Understanding who we write for is as important as understanding what we write. Side Quest Syndicate has a core audience and a secondary audience. We write primarily for the core audience, with the secondary audience in mind as people we want to bring into the conversation.

## Primary Audience: The Dedicated Player

**Who they are.** Adults aged 22–38 who play games seriously but not professionally. They hold jobs, have social lives, and have limited gaming time — which makes them selective. They read reviews before buying, follow gaming news but are sceptical of hype cycles, and feel underserved by both mainstream gaming outlets (too corporate) and hyper-niche communities (too insular).

**What they play.** Their backlog is a source of genuine anxiety. They tend toward games with depth — RPGs, strategy games, action-adventures with strong writing, and indie titles with distinct design visions. They complete most games they start and have strong opinions about the ones they do not finish. They play on at least two platforms (typically PC plus one console).

**How they consume content.** They read long-form content. They will spend 20 minutes with a thoughtful review if it earns that time. They subscribe to newsletters from writers they trust. They listen to gaming podcasts during commutes. They are active in Discord communities but do not post constantly — they read more than they write.

**What they want from Side Quest Syndicate.** Honest criticism they can trust before spending money or time. Context that helps them understand a game's place in its genre. A community that debates seriously without devolving into toxicity. Writing that respects their intelligence and does not waste their time.

**What they can get elsewhere and why they stay here.** IGN and GameSpot give them scores and coverage breadth but not depth or honesty. Reddit gives them community but not curated quality. Substack writers give them opinions but not news coverage. Side Quest Syndicate gives them the combination: editorial quality, community depth, and an authentic voice. That is the value proposition.

## Secondary Audience: The Curious Newcomer

**Who they are.** Players aged 18–26 who are deepening their engagement with games for the first time. They may have grown up with mobile games or casual console titles and are now exploring the broader gaming landscape. They found Side Quest Syndicate via social media, a recommendation, or a guide that ranked well in search.

**What they play.** Popular titles that have strong word-of-mouth in wider culture — games like Elden Ring, Baldur's Gate 3, Stardew Valley, or whatever is culturally prominent at the time. They are building their gaming vocabulary. They are not embarrassed about what they do not know yet.

**How they consume content.** They come in through search (guides), stay for the community, and may eventually become core audience members. They engage more on social platforms (TikTok, Instagram, YouTube Shorts) than on long-form reading surfaces. They are more likely to share a post than to comment on it.

**What they want from Side Quest Syndicate.** Practical help (guides), accessible criticism that does not assume encyclopaedic game knowledge, and a community that welcomes newcomers without condescension.

## Platform Preferences

**Discord.** Our most engaged platform. The core audience lives here. This is where real-time discussion, community polls, and game nights happen. New content is announced here first for members.

**Newsletter.** Our highest-trust surface. Open rates above 40% indicate our newsletter subscribers are highly engaged. Long-form essays and reviews that we are most proud of get newsletter-first previews. We do not publish more than two issues per week.

**Website.** The canonical home for all content. SEO-optimised but not SEO-compromised — we write for readers, not crawlers. The archives are a product in themselves: years of trustworthy criticism that maintains value over time.

**Twitter / X.** Breaking news, hot takes, and community conversation. We maintain a presence but do not optimise for this platform's engagement dynamics. We do not post threads designed to go viral. We post opinions and links.

**Instagram.** Visual content: screenshots, infographics summarising review scores, community event announcements. Secondary platform — we maintain quality but do not over-invest.

**YouTube.** Video essays and review supplements for major titles. Low cadence, high quality. We do not compete with video-first channels. YouTube content complements the written work rather than replacing it.

## Audience Pain Points We Solve

**Review trust deficit.** Many players no longer trust major outlets after years of inflated scores and publisher relationships. Side Quest Syndicate's transparency policy and track record directly address this.

**Time scarcity.** Our core audience has limited gaming time. A bad purchase stings more than it used to. Our reviews help them make confident decisions. Our guides help them not lose hours to friction the game should have explained.

**Community quality.** Most gaming communities trend toward toxicity at scale. Side Quest Syndicate's moderated Discord with clear community standards provides a rare alternative: serious discussion without harassment.

**Depth gap.** The market for 300-word gaming news is saturated. There is less competition and more reader loyalty at the 1,500–3,000 word level. Our core audience actively seeks depth — they will find us if we produce it consistently.
