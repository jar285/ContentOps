# Side Quest Syndicate — Brand Identity

Side Quest Syndicate is a gaming content brand built for players who treat every session as an adventure worth talking about. We cover the games that matter to real players — from indie darlings to AAA blockbusters — with an honest voice, genuine enthusiasm, and zero corporate polish.

## Mission Statement

Our mission is to be the co-op partner every gamer wishes they had: knowledgeable, enthusiastic, and always down to dive deep. We exist to turn gaming moments into conversation — helping players discover what to play next, understand what they just experienced, and connect with a community that takes games seriously without taking itself too seriously.

We believe that games are one of the most expressive art forms of the modern era, and that they deserve criticism, coverage, and celebration that matches their ambition. Side Quest Syndicate is where that coverage lives.

## Core Values

**Authenticity over hype.** We never inflate a game's score to chase traffic or bury a criticism because a publisher is an advertiser. Our reviews are written by people who finished the game, not people who played the tutorial.

**Depth over breadth.** We would rather publish one excellent 2,000-word review than six shallow 300-word takes. Our audience is smart and they can tell the difference. We respect their time by making every piece worth it.

**Community first.** The Syndicate is not a publication that happens to have comments. It is a community that happens to produce content. Our Discord, our community posts, and our comment sections are as important as our editorial output.

**Transparency in process.** We publish our review scoring rubric, our conflict-of-interest policy, and our correction history openly. When we get something wrong, we say so in public.

**Inclusivity without compromise.** We write for everyone who loves games. We do not tolerate harassment, gatekeeping, or exclusion in our community spaces.

## Brand Voice

Side Quest Syndicate sounds like a knowledgeable friend at a gaming table — not a press release, not a podcast ad-read, and not a lecture. Our voice is:

**Conversational.** We write like we talk. Short sentences mix with longer ones. We use contractions. We are not afraid of a fragment for emphasis.

**Specific.** Vague praise is meaningless. We name the mechanic, the moment, the level, the boss fight. "The combat is great" is never enough. "The parry window on the second-phase transition of the Hollow King fight is tight enough to feel earned but forgiving enough to stay fun" — that is what we write.

**Confident but not arrogant.** We back our opinions with reasoning, but we acknowledge when something is a matter of taste. We say "I found the pacing slow in act two" not "the pacing is objectively broken."

**Enthusiastic without being breathless.** We love games. That comes through. But we do not use words like "incredible," "insane," or "mind-blowing" in the first paragraph of every review. Reserve the superlatives for when they are earned.

## Visual Identity

**Color palette.** Deep navy (#1A2340) as primary background, electric teal (#00D4AA) as accent, warm white (#F5F0E8) for body text, and signal red (#FF4444) for alerts and warnings. The palette reads as focused and modern without feeling cold.

**Typography.** Headlines use a condensed grotesque (we reference Barlow Condensed for weight and attitude). Body text uses a legible serif at reading size — we take long-form content seriously and our typography shows it. Code and game titles use a monospaced face to distinguish them from editorial text.

**Logo.** The wordmark pairs a bold sans-serif "SIDE QUEST" with a smaller-weight "SYNDICATE" beneath. The icon is an eight-pointed compass rose — suggesting exploration, direction, and community navigation.

## What We Are Not

We are not a news aggregator. We do not repackage press releases or chase embargo-day traffic. If we publish about a game announcement, we have a point of view on it.

We are not an influencer network. Our writers are not "content creators" optimising for engagement metrics. They are writers and critics optimising for quality.

We are not platform agnostic in a cowardly sense. We cover all platforms but we have opinions about them. We are not afraid to say that a game is better on PC, that a port was rushed, or that a platform holder made a bad policy call.

We are not corporate. We do not have a PR department. We do not issue statements. When something goes wrong, the editor writes a post and we talk about it with the community.
