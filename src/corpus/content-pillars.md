# Side Quest Syndicate — Content Pillars

Our content is built on five pillars. Every piece we publish falls primarily under one of these categories. Understanding the pillars helps writers know what angle to take, helps editors know what is missing from the calendar, and helps the audience know what to expect from us.

## Pillar 1: Reviews and Critical Coverage

**What it is.** Honest, scored reviews of games across all platforms, plus retrospective re-evaluations of older titles. This is the backbone of Side Quest Syndicate's editorial identity.

**What it covers.** New releases (published within embargo or within one week of launch), early access updates that represent a significant content milestone, ports and remasters evaluated on their own merits versus the original release, and annual anniversary re-reviews for culturally significant games.

**Example post formats.**
- Full scored review (1,500–3,000 words) with a verdict summary, scored rubric, and one-paragraph TLDR at the top.
- "Now That The Hype Has Settled" re-evaluation (800–1,200 words) published six months after a divisive launch.
- Port report: a focused 600–900 word piece comparing the new version to the source.

**Success metrics.** Review completion rate (did readers reach the verdict?), search ranking for "[game name] review", referral traffic from gaming aggregators, and time-on-page above 3 minutes.

## Pillar 2: Guides and Strategy

**What it is.** Practical, specific help for players who are stuck, optimising, or trying to understand a system. Guides are evergreen — they are designed to be useful six months after publication, not just launch week.

**What it covers.** Boss fight strategies, progression tips for complex RPG systems, collectible location guides, build recommendations for character/class-based games, and "how does X mechanic actually work" explainers for systems the game itself explains poorly.

**Example post formats.**
- Boss guide (400–800 words): setup recommendations, phase breakdown, common mistakes, and a summary tip box.
- System explainer (600–1,000 words): explaining a game's economy, crafting tree, or faction system in plain language.
- Build guide (800–1,500 words): recommended stat allocations, gear priorities, and skill order with reasoning for each choice.

**Success metrics.** Organic search traffic, return visits (guides should rank and keep working), low bounce rate (readers are actively using the guide while playing), and Discord mentions ("the Syndicate guide helped me").

## Pillar 3: News and Industry Commentary

**What it is.** Reporting on and analysis of gaming industry events — announcements, controversies, business moves, and platform news — with a clear editorial point of view. We do not just report; we interpret.

**What it covers.** Game announcements with genuine reader interest, studio acquisitions and their likely impact on franchises, pricing and business model controversies (loot boxes, DLC practices, subscription model changes), platform policy changes, and layoff coverage with context about industry conditions.

**Example post formats.**
- News brief with take (300–500 words): what happened, why it matters, what we think.
- Analysis piece (800–1,200 words): a longer look at a trend or decision with historical context and a clear thesis.
- Community reaction roundup (400–600 words): what players are saying and why it reflects something real.

**Success metrics.** Social shares and engagement, newsletter open rate for news issues, and Discord discussion threads spawned by the piece.

## Pillar 4: Community Features

**What it is.** Content that puts the Syndicate community at the centre — highlighting member perspectives, running community-driven features, and celebrating the culture of gaming itself rather than any single product.

**What it covers.** Community spotlight interviews with interesting members, reader-submitted gaming stories, community polls with editorial analysis, "what are you playing this weekend" weekly threads, and retrospective discussions driven by Discord consensus.

**Example post formats.**
- Member spotlight (500–800 words): an interview with a community member about their gaming history, current obsessions, or a specific expertise.
- Community poll result piece (400–600 words): we run a poll, the community votes, we analyse the data and share the interesting patterns.
- "This week in the Discord" digest (300–500 words): a curated summary of the week's best discussions, debates, and discoveries from the community space.

**Success metrics.** Community participation rate in polls and threads, Discord membership growth, share rate of spotlight pieces (members share content they appear in), and comment count on community pieces.

## Pillar 5: Long-Form and Essayistic Writing

**What it is.** The work we are most proud of — original essays, investigative pieces, and long-form criticism that treats games as culture worth serious analysis. This is what distinguishes Side Quest Syndicate from a content mill.

**What it covers.** Critical essays on a single game's themes or design decisions, cross-game analysis comparing how different titles handle the same problem, industry investigations (requiring sourcing and reporting), and personal essays about what gaming means — loss, community, identity, escapism.

**Example post formats.**
- Critical essay (1,500–3,500 words): a focused argument about a game's meaning, design, or cultural impact.
- Comparative analysis (1,200–2,500 words): two or more games examined through a shared lens — how both handle grief, how both design their economies, how both construct player agency.
- Personal essay (800–2,000 words): first-person writing about gaming experience, with the editorial bar of any good personal essay: it must say something true beyond just the writer's experience.

**Success metrics.** Newsletter sharing rate, social media pick-up, links from external publications, and qualitative reader response (emails and comments that engage with the argument, not just the game).
